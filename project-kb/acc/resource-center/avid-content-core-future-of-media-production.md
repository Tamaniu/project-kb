# Avid Content Core — The Future of Media Production

**Source:** Avid Resource Center — <https://www.avid.com/resource-center/avid-content-core-the-future-of-media-production>
**Published:** 17/11/2025
**Primary voice:** Tom Sharma, Avid Chief Technology Officer (joined March 2025; previously Hulu, NBCUniversal)

---

## Summary — Why It Matters (per source page)

- **Single pane of glass:** Track assets from origination through distribution and performance
- **Cloud-agnostic and hybrid-ready:** Works across AWS, GCP, Azure, on-premises, and multi-cloud environments
- **AI-powered automation:** Speeds up editorial processes, freeing creatives to focus on storytelling
- **Open and interoperable:** API-first architecture aligns with the MovieLabs 2030 vision

---

## CTO Positioning

> "Avid Content Core is the foundation layer for future media workflows. It is going to be a game changer."
> — **Tom Sharma, Avid Chief Technology Officer**

Sharma frames Avid Content Core as addressing the silos that have historically separated content creation, distribution, and monetisation:

> "There's media creation inside of the house that actually produces media. Then there's another set of folks that actually monetize and distribute it, and then there's yet another set of folks that actually buy the media on the advertising side of the house. So, there were silos. Those walls are definitely coming down, and if they can get further insights, that helps monetize the content better."
> — **Tom Sharma**

---

## Platform Capabilities

### Content and Metadata Management
Avid Content Core manages and indexes content and metadata across on-premises, cloud, and hybrid environments, enabling teams to search, access, and collaborate without concern for where the media physically resides.

> "It basically manages and indexes content and metadata across on-prem, cloud, and hybrid, so teams can search, access, and collaborate without worrying where the media is."
> — **Tom Sharma**

### AI and Automation
AI is positioned explicitly as an enabler, not a replacement for craft:

> "It's about how we give the editors and the storytellers more time so that they can focus on creativity and less on chasing and preparing media. How do you take raw media and how fast can you create finished stories so you can allow the networks and the studios to monetize that media?"
> — **Tom Sharma**

**Practical example — Quickture:** The Quickture panel in Media Composer uses AI to generate a basic roughcut from interview footage. On a recent reality TV series, this enabled the production team to deliver approximately three times as many casting tapes for consideration.

### Open API and Interoperability

> "API is not just about integration today. They are absolutely the foundation of interoperability and AI-driven workflows of tomorrow."
> — **Tom Sharma**

API-first architecture is described as the foundation for both current integrations and future agentic AI workflows. The approach explicitly aligns with the **MovieLabs 2030 vision**: applications come to the media, rather than media coming to the applications.

### Multi-Cloud and Cloud-Agnostic Architecture

Avid Content Core is built on AWS and leverages AWS technologies, but Sharma is explicit that the platform is cloud-agnostic and multi-cloud:

> "Avid Content Core is cloud-agnostic. AWS is a fantastic partner. The bigger story here is how we're developing an open foundation that works across the ecosystem. Our products are very clearly multi-cloud, including on-prem. We really are aligning ourselves to the MovieLabs 2030 vision, which means the apps come to the media, and the media doesn't have to come to the apps. So, if you're using GCP and that's where your media is, we'll come to the GCP where your media sits. We will index it and make it searchable."
> — **Tom Sharma**

Confirmed cloud environments: **AWS**, **GCP**, **Azure**, on-premises, multi-cloud hybrid.

**Cloud partnerships noted:**
- AWS: partnership signed for cloud-based storage and post-production workflows with Media Composer and Avid NEXIS — see <https://www.avid.com/avid-on-aws>
- Microsoft Azure: existing partnership for cloud workflows

## Monetisation as a Core Theme

A recurring theme throughout the article is that Avid Content Core is designed not just to manage production workflows but to enable customers to generate revenue from the content they create. This represents a deliberate shift in Avid's positioning — from workflow tooling to a platform that participates in the wealth-creating activities of its customers across the full production-to-monetisation supply chain.
