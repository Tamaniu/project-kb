# Navigation, Display Preferences and Accessibility

Wolftech aims to offer the most inclusive experience to make sure your whole team feels at home in the app and can collaborate efficiently. As such we have worked hard to be compliant with the highest industry standard for accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AAA) to make your navigation as smooth as possible.

## Devices

Wolftech shines across very different devices offering the possibility to configure them to help you focus and do your best work.

### Web

If you are working on a wide or multi screen setup or a laptop the web browser version will be the right choice for you. Available from anywhere without any app installs.

### Mobile

The Mobile app, Wolftech Go, has all of the same feature rich functionality but it is customised for a more mobile experience that leverages all of the functionality from your mobile phone and works great across iOS and Android, on mobiles or tablets. The tablet experience of Wolftech Go automatically resizes to your screen size to make the most of your real estate.

For more information on the mobile app please check the Wolftech Go Guide. This guide will focus mainly on the web experience.

## Light vs Dark Modes

The most used setting for customising your experience of Wolftech News is the light vs Dark modes.

Dark mode is great for working later in the evening or in dark spaces while light mode is perfect for open office environments with lots of light. Whatever your preference we have 2 gorgeously designed modes for you that can be switched on and off from the top right menu.

## Advanced Web Setup

In the top right corner you will be able to access the My Page Menu. Here is where you will be able to make most of the changes to the UI to make sure it displays and works just the way you need it too.

### Language

You can choose the language for the entire application in the My Page – Settings Page.

Currently we support English, German, French, Spanish, Norwegian, Danish, Swedish, Dutch, Catalan and Japanese but do ask your project manager if other languages are required.

Make sure to check with your internal lead as there could well be a specific language file customised for your organisation so all buttons and actions are called just the way you need them to.

Note: The language preference will have to be chosen as well on the mobile app, however if your organisation has a custom language file it may come preloaded once you log in.

### Time

The clock will display on the top right at all times so you never miss a deadline. The default is 24 hs but in the my page settings you can set it to 12hs if that feels more comfortable.

### UI Options (Comfortable Mode)

Overall, Wolftech allows you to customise the way the UI presents the information. In the wide range of users we have, each will feel a combination will work best for them.

Some people may prefer to increase the contrast of the application, some can take advantage of widening the space between each letter for readability or enlarge the size of the target areas to create more breathing space, whilst others benefit from presenting more information at a glance in a compact approach.

Whatever your combination of preference this can be set in my page settings.

We also acknowledge that you may not want to use these settings all of the time and there are certain situations where easily turning them on and off may be helpful so we have added a "Comfortable mode" submenu to the top right menu so you can toggle them as needed.

### Colours and High Contrast

Overall, Wolftech takes care to make sure all colour and visual indicators have equivalent readable lists. As an example, the department that created or is collaborating on a story will be displayed as a sidebar but can also be seen from the department field.

Colour sidebars also have a minimum width to make it easier to see them.

For some of our users a higher contrast can be helpful to focus on the content so we have enabled this option in the my page settings.

It addresses both the contrast ratio of text and non text elements, including tabbing. And colours are carefully chosen for colour blind users too.

If you need to see the system in greyscale this can be enabled as a system option in Mac or Windows via their accessibility tools and our interface will successfully interpret the mode in greyscale.

If you need to change the overall UI colour theme for your computer to aid with readability In Windows 11 you can go to Settings, then Personalisation, then Colours. There are a lot of options and different themes to choose from.

### Font Spacing

Some users prefer to opt for a wider letter spacing to improve the readability of the text. This can be easily turned on from the My Page – Settings section.

The results of wider letter spacing can be seen by comparing normal and expanded spacing modes.

### UI Density (Large Target Areas)

UI Density is another aspect that matters a lot to some users who like to have more padding across all cards and buttons to make it easier to see everything as well as to make it easier to target a button with the cursor.

Wolftech has an overall design for the main buttons to have a minimum size to make it easier to work with it. However we want to have an option to make the entire application "breathe" a bit more for our users.

In the settings options you will be able to choose between the default compact mode that allows you to see more information at a glance and the "large target areas" option that provides larger cards and buttons.

## Reactive Design

### Zooming and Scaling

Wolftech has been designed to support zooming in to scale your display up to 200% at 1920x1080. The whole application will react to make sure you have the best experience without having to scroll left or right to see the information.

The top menu will become vertical and all information will be easily accessible throughout. Left and right panels will become overlays that you can open and close. The left menu will get top and bottom arrows to navigate through its options too.

Note there are exceptions on pages that are designed as tables (like production timelines) which are meant to be scrolled left to right like the booking page.

### Adjustable Columns

The left menu can be kept closed or open. You can also control the left column width by dragging the handle to determine the preferred width.

The story right panel also has a column that can be expanded as needed.

Do make it yours. The setting will be remembered throughout your session and even after reloading the page.

For users with extra large screens, the activity feed can be docked to the right of the right column so you can continue working on your story while keeping track of all of the latest updates across your team's work.

### Pins and Alerts Adjustable Height

Over in the bottom left corner you will find your saved (Pinned) items as well as all of the alerts. These panels are also reactive and can be dragged up and and down.

### Sticky Headers

In sections where a lot of content may be written we have made the top and bottoms "sticky" so you always have access to the menus. You can see examples of these when you scroll down through a story, or in long background information boxes or in long publishing points. Rest assured your navigation will help you keep in focus.

### Kanban vs List Views

Both the plan and the feed pages can be seen in kanban vs list views. Check which one fits your style best.

### Focus

On Plan view or Feed view columns we have designed a feature to always keep your column in focus when you scroll down through the elements. Scroll up to see new items that may have been added to the column knowing that your readability will always be kept in focus.

## Keyboard Navigation

### Tabbing

The UI can be fully explored using tabbing to go to the next element in the UI. Use Shift+Tab to go backwards instead of forwards.

Tab Focus highlights will help you determine where you are in the application to help you in your navigation. It has a consistent design to make it as frictionless as possible and enough width for all of our users.

Do note the high contrast mode has an increased tabbing highlight so if you prefer to have better definition of the tiles, cards, buttons and tabbing please switch on the high contrast mode.

### Opening Sections

Clicking Enter or Spacebar after you focus the tabbing on a section will open the section/menu/column you are in. To make it easier to navigate we will also move your focus into the open section or column so you can continue to navigate inside of it easily.

### Headings and Groups

If a page has multiple sections or groups with headings they will always have a collapse or expanded button.

If you collapse the button you will be able to navigate to the next group header that contains any content. If you have it expanded you will be able to navigate through the elements inside of the group. Pressing escape will navigate back to the group for ease of use.

### List View Navigation in Plan and Feeds View

- From the left column you can tab to move through them
- Enter will load the column to see the feeds without navigating into the feeds centre pane
- Hitting the spacebar in a column will both load the column and land you on the first feed in that column
- Hitting the spacebar on a feed will pop up the expanded view for that feed
- Using up and down arrows you can move through the feeds either in list or expanded
- Hitting the escape will navigate out of the expanded view
- Hitting the left arrow will take you back to the column navigator

### Tag Selectors

Much of the categories for each element (Story, event, resources, etc.) have a search component and some option to single or multi select tags. To open the category menu use the return button.

When opened you will land directly on the search field from which you can type a search. With the tabbing you can navigate to the different options matching the search and select them with the space bar.

### Date Pickers

Date pickers will land automatically on the first date field where you can type your search. To add a secondary date tab and select add multiple dates and you will be on the second date field. And will move you into the addition of a secondary date automatically for ease of navigation. The latter can be tabbed to skip the option and simply save the dates.

Once you are happy with the date tab through the date cell, delete and cancel all the way to save.

For time ranges date pickers will put the start date into focus automatically and when entered just use the tab to move to the end date and then tab through cancel onto save to save your selection.

## Icons and Tooltips

It is easy to get familiarised with the icons across the application in the Icons Legend also located in the Help/About Section.

Whenever in doubt about icons or buttons across the application simply hover over them to display the relevant tooltip.

## Screen Reader

Wolftech has been designed to support screen reader applications that allow our customers to read all elements of the application to both navigate and utilise it fully without vision. It is constantly tested for compatibility with JAWS and our teams of active daily screen reader users among our clients provide us with helpful real life feedback that is constantly actioned.

Tabbing through the application will allow you to focus on the right area of the application. The buttons will be read to you. When you navigate inside of menus you can use the up and down arrows to navigate through the options.

All of the text is available to the language files and as such can be localised to follow your custom language file.

## AI Summaries

AI Summaries can be helpful when trying to get up to speed with big stories. You can trigger summaries using our scout function across the editorial section as well as for Datamnir event tracking.
