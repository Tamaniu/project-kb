# Greenlight

## Intro

Greenlighting a story or episode is sometimes required and can be done by a subset of people with those correct permissions. The process of greenlight can be as simple or involved as admins decide to make it.

The greenlight process can have technical checks as well as organisational operational checks that are not defined in the system. Generally the production greenlight process is more involved than the story greenlight process.

## Operational Greenlight Checks

Beyond the content categorisation where you can define which elements are required before greenlight including genres, production types, descriptions, the correct department level or additional information, optionally you may enforce other checks like:

- Adding at least one publishing point to know where you plan to publish the content
- Going through a budget check in additional information
- Adding a budget code
- Deciding on which optional stages are required for the episode
- Reviewing the timeline
- Creating the Story/episode PAM/MAM collection

Once you are happy with the project readiness if you have the permissions you will be able to greenlight it.

## Greenlight Progress

If you don't see the greenlight button it is because you are missing some items. Clicking on the greenlight progress bar will reveal the missing actions.

For additional information be sure to check any fields that have the red dot as this will indicate their mandatory nature.

## Greenlight Notification and Summary

Once you click on the greenlight button it will mark the project as greenlit and will notify all booked resources and defined greenlight contacts for the department that the production is now greenlit.

It will also send a greenlight summary email to all people booked on it.

In the "My Page" section you can check your personal settings for this notification.
