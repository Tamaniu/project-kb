# Content Categorisation

## Intro

Once a story or production has been created you can categorise it in different ways to make sure the right people can find it later. All project categories enable others to filter searches or plan views to display just the right content they are after.

Categories include departments, offices, dates, deadlines, unique IDs, genres/Tags, or Production types (Formats). The latter will also define any custom metadata forms for your project.

## Team Categories

Who is working on a project determines who gets to access the project so it is of utmost importance.

### Private vs Public Project

A project may start as private, meaning only the person who creates it sees it. To make it private you can do so in the quick add or after project creation.

From there that person can give access to named collaborators via the story or episode permissions under the 3-dot menu.

**Note:** When converting a private pitch to a story or episode you will be asked if you want to keep it private. There is an exception to this when you have the "Enable permission inheritance when converting note to story" admin option enabled.

### Department(s) and Office(s)

Once the right privacy level has been set you can choose which department and office will be working on your project.

These are 2 of the most important categories in the system as they will make your story visible to your colleagues. For efficiency, the project main department is auto-selected by your default department. The same goes for the office.

**Note:** Episodes cannot be shared with other departments. Departments collaborate on stories and a particular department or brand is in charge of a long form episode that may be linked to the story.

## Timed-Based Categories

Defining when a story or production happens determines whether those projects are shown or hidden from planning calendar views in the left menu or in the plan view so it is critical to get it right.
