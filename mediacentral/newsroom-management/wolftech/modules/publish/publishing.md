# Publishing

## Publishing Overview

### Plan View

The Plan view publishing dashboards commonly track all the output for the platforms that you manage. The left menu can be opened to display the stories of the day out of which you can plan your publishing activation simply by dragging and dropping the story cards into one of the publishing columns.

Publishing point filters allow you to create these views. The primary filter is the Publishing folder, allowing you to determine the Publishing Destinations you would like to target in your column.

You have the option to show the hierarchy folder on top of your publishing destination. This can be useful when you want to track total run time within the hierarchy folder but can take unnecessary space if you don't need that.

You can also set up the filter to display a specific status to track the progress of publishing content across columns. Use the filter by templates to split the dashboard between materials vs reports, for example.

Additional options include filtering by genre, office or source story department. The default sorting of publishing column is by time, meaning intended publication time. Sorting can also be manual (to sort them by priority or your defined order). Shows with a fixed start time can be sorted manually regardless of the sorting option. Finally sorting can also be done by last modified so you get the last modified publishing outputs on top.

Grouping can be done by Publishing folder (default) or by Office. Do note however than when grouping by office the sort time will default to sort by time, manual sorting is not possible.

### Story Publishing List

Inside of each story, In the publishing tab you can see, edit, and add publishing points for your story or production. The publishing tab will display all associated publishing points for your story or production.

In the tab it will also display the total amount of pub points at the top, like we do for bookings.

### Story Publishing Schedule

On the Right menu you can also check the publishing plan for your story for the today.

### Create

To add a new one simply click on the + button and select a folder and select a date and time for it.

To undo your selection, click on the x next to the selected platform at the bottom.

The publishing point will take the name of your story or production by default, to rename it click on the icon of the publishing point. You can also just open it by click on its title and in the window click on the title again to edit the title.

ProTip: You can favourite certain publishing destinations. This will show you only your favourites next time you try to create an output. Simply uncheck the display favourites only option if you want to see all destinations.

Creating a Publishing point is also possible from the Plan view. You can drag a story tile, or an episode tile for long form, from the left menu into a publishing column to create a publishing point for that publishing folder.

If multiple options are available in that column, you will have a popup window to select it after dragging it.

Note: If the Pub point column has a status filter, it will tag it with the column's status. If you try to drop it on a status beyond a step that requires approval it will error out to make sure you don't sidetrack the workflow template.

## Publishing Editor

### User Experience and Navigation

The publishing editor has several sections that is important to familiarise yourself with.

On the left you can see a number of icons representing the publishing plan for the story you are in. Clicking on those items will open each output so you can edit them. At the top of the publishing plan you will find the story icon. You can click on it to navigate to the story.

On top of the story icon, you will find an arrow to expand the publishing point plan and see more information. The system will remember your last used settings so next time you open a publishing output it will have the publishing plan collapsed or expanded depending on it.

In the centre top you will find the publishing Header. Here you can see:

- The Publishing Title (editable if you click on it)
- The Publishing Unique ID (That can be copied)
- The Publishing Status that can be adjusted
- The ability to follow the content
- A 3-dot menu for further actions
- Below you will find the publishing schedule (i.e. when and where it will be published)

As you scroll down the header will become sticky and will show less information.

In the centre middle section below the header, you will find the publishing tabs:

- The editor tab – to edit and publish the content
- The details tab – where you can add a description, additional information and manage guidance requests
- The bookings tab – where you can book or request resources to deliver
- A notes tab to add a long list of notes

In the editor tab you will find the editor itself which is broken into 2 sections:

- A Form Panel on the left (which can be collapsed and hidden by default)
- A content (Script Editor) section to the right

The fields and actions of these subpanels depend on the type of publishing you are working on.

On the right section you will see the Right Panel. The right panel can be collapsed and opened by clicking on the icons on the right. Its width can also be adjusted via drag. The X button closes the whole publishing modal.

The right panel includes:

- A message thread to discuss and collaborate on all aspects of publishing
- An activity log – to check on all the activity updates for this publishing point
- A Board – to select media to be published
- A MAM media search panel

### Editor

The content to be published is packaged in the editor leveraging specific templates for each destination.

**Choosing a Template**

Your publishing destination may use one or more templates. If there is only one template it will be loaded. If multiple ones are available, you will need to choose one before you can start adding content.

**Locking and Collaboration**

With a template to work with you will be able to enter the details of your publishing output by simply clicking on any of the fields in the template. The system has individual field locking level allowing multiple users to edit different fields in the template at the same time.

**Importing Text or Data from Your Story**

Your template may already map some information from your story to your publishing point automatically. Like the title above.

To import text from your story, use the import content button. Select a source from your story you will be able to preview the source. If importing content from your editorial segments you can filter the Segments by tab and its status.

You can also import the name and description of staff or guests in your story (Perfect for graphic workflows in publishing).

*Note that you cannot import guests (Contacts) that have been added as reference Contacts instead of booked.*

Once the source is selected you will have 3 options, canceling the import, importing it as it (Add) or using the smart publishing point function.

**Publishing Assistant (Smart Publishing)**

The Smart Pub Point function will open the AI assistant where you can select the type of smart assistance required to transform your source text (Social Media Post vs Translation, etc.). Select the type of within your function (Facebook) and then hit create.

Once the prompt is selected you will be able to preview it before import. Always keeping you in the loop. Click import once completed knowing that you can always continue to edit it in your template before publishing.

**Importing Media**

Most publishing templates include a variety of fields for copy and media. Selected Media can come from variety of sources:

A) Your own device
B) The story board
C) The publishing board
D) A MAM Media Search

Adding Media from your own device is as easy as double clicking on the media field you want to target. This will both add it to your media field and upload it to the story board.

You can also drag media from the board or the story board directly on to the media field in the template to select it.

Once images, audios or videos are added from the board, you should be able to preview them from the template.

You can also drag MAM Assets from the media search panel to a compatible template field.

After you drop the asset on the field you will not be able to preview them from the template but a reference to the asset with a hyperlink to it will be on display.

Media selections are auto saved with no further action needed to confirm their selection.

**Assigning a Presenter**

If your template has a Presenter type field you will be able to search from the list of presenters in Wolftech and add it. This will determine the reference reading speed for your read time calculations.

**Tracking Read Time**

The editor allows to track:

- TTC: Time to Cursor, calculated for all fields labelled as Script editor up to the point of your cursor
- BLK: Block Time, calculating all of the reading time for the section highlighted
- Read Time: Calculating the read time for all script editor fields in your template

**Changing Templates and Mapping**

If you need to change the publishing point template of an existing publishing point you can do so without losing its data (mapping the data to the new chosen template).

If you have a publishing point where you have already worked on adding the metadata:

When you change the template you will be presented with an option to start the new publishing point from scratch or convert the data to match the new template.

All fields where there is a matching field title will be mapped automatically but you will have a chance to manually adjust mapping as needed for those where there is no match.

### Publishing Workflow

A publishing point will go through a publishing workflow to determine the steps and approvals required to publish to a platform. You can check this in the publishing point list, and it will display in all the plan views for tracking. Clicking on it allows you to change it and see when it was created and last modified.

You can adjust the status in the top right with the drop down or by clicking the next button.

If an approval is required in the workflow template, you will either not be able to move it beyond its status or see an approve button. Clicking on the approve will move it to the next stage.

Once you reach a stage where the next action is to publish, you will see the publish button to the left of the status.

Note, after this point the publishing metadata and content will be published to the 3rd party platform and the status of the publishing point will move to the next stage. Failure or success of publication will come in a notification.

### Details Tab

**Description and Additional Information**

In the details tab the first field you will find is the description field that can be mapped to the story description or the previous publishing point to automatically populate it.

Depending on your admin setup certain publishing destinations may have extra metadata fields that you can use for organising your publishing work. These custom fields live in the Additional information section.

**Guidance**

Below Additional Information you can request guidance from legal, rights & clearances etc. on a publishing point. See Guidance guide for more info. All conversations will be logged in to understand how the request was resolved.

**Bookings**

You can add contacts for reference to a publishing point. This is not a booking, and they will not be notified of it, but it can be useful to know which specialist is part of your publishing point.

You can also book guests to the publishing point which will allow you to track if they are coming into the building and the history of relationships for that guest with the organisation across stories.

You can book team members to collaborate on a publishing point if it is specific to its delivery and separate from the story or production.

Finally, you can request people, equipment etc. for your publishing point.

Note – see admin section because you can define to automatically assign the publishing point ownership to the first staff booking that you make.

### Notes

The notes tab lets you add useful information for all teams working on the publishing point. It has the same 4,000-character limit as the description field but does not require the Froala editor styling capabilities.

The notes field now has a character count to let you know when you are about to reach the 4,000-character limit and will prevent you from trying to continue to write beyond its maximum capacity. If you try to write or paste a text longer than its maximum character limitation it will prevent you from continuing to write.

## Communication and Collaboration

### Chat

The publishing point has its own message thread for all specific chats about its publishing. All mentions, reply in threads, likes, etc. work just the same way.

This right panel can be dragged to be wider to make the best use of it.

### Activity Log

The publishing point's activity log tracks the editorial activity inside of a pub point. This includes status changes, description changes, additional information changes, changes to the template editor (content) and comments.

Note: It does not include Publishing point title changes.

Users can choose when to notify of changes to the additional information fields by clicking on the log activity. This will give you a message that the activity has been successfully logged and those that click on the log will be directed to see the changes.

Users will also be able to manually log activity changes to the template in the editor with the same Log Activity button. This appears when you unlock the template for editing.

Note, in this instance you can make multiple changes to the template, and we will just notify you that the template has been updated.

In the description field, like on the editorial segments, saving the change will update the activity log but you can also save without logging for correction on typos.

### Follow, Notifications and the Activity Feed

The owner of the publishing point or those booked on it will be notified of changes to its status.

Other team members can follow a publishing point to receive updates with any editorial changes to it. This includes changes to, updates from the additional information section, changes to the publishing template, comments, or changes in status.

The activity will be presented in the activity Feed. See the Communication and collaboration guide for more information.

To change the notification preferences for publishing point changes, go to your My Page – Notifications menu.

### Read and Activity Updated Indicators in Pub Points

To help you navigate the increasing amount of content output and focus on what matters, when you are planning your publishing calendar, we will give you 2 indicators to check:

- New Publishing points you have never accessed (Unread) – Orange dot
- Publishing points that have been updated since the last time you accessed them – orange triangle

With the orange triangle on hover over you will be able to see the last change and from there you can click to dismiss the update on the triangle or click on the publishing point to see the full activity log.

No more chasing around for updates.

### Sharing a Publishing Point

If you need to share the details of the pub point you can do so in multiple ways.

An easy way is to share a link to the publishing point. This can be found in the 3-dot menu, and it is a great option for people with access to Wolftech.

Alternatively, you may want to share the Publishing Point Unique ID. This can be found on the pub point under the title and can be copied and may be helpful if you need to find this ID in a 3rd party system.

The ID can also easily get it from the main publishing point list.

Finally, you can print the Publishing point as a PDF and share the file. Great for people who don't have access to Wolftech or who may be working in a remote area without internet access.

When you print you can specify if they want to include:

- the description
- the additional Information
- Content Guidance
- The template elements of the publishing point
