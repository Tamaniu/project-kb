# The Benefits of a Story-Centric Approach

**Source:** Avid Resource Center — <https://www.avid.com/resource-center/the-benefits-of-a-story-centric-approach>
**Published:** 25/03/2026

> Shifting to a story-centric operating model can supercharge a news organisation, but how do you
> start such a transformation?

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Transformation is necessary, but hard:** Audience shifts to digital, declining trust, and cost pressures make newsroom transformation unavoidable — yet cultural resistance and poor change management cause most transformations to fail.
- **Story-centric models enable modernisation:** Moving from siloed teams to a unified, story-first workflow allows newsrooms to work more efficiently, supporting both digital growth and linear broadcast.
- **Collaboration and single-pane tools drive efficiency:** Platforms like Wolftech News and MediaCentral centralise planning, communication, scripting, and asset access — reducing duplication, improving field-to-newsroom coordination, and saving time.
- **Automation and reuse boost output without disruption:** Automated versioning with human oversight, multiplatform publishing, and shared media help teams produce more stories faster, modernise incrementally, and stay competitive.

---

## Industry Context and Statistics

### Cost and Transformation Pressure
- **DPP Media CTO Survey:** Cost reduction was a top strategic priority for **65%** of respondents.
- **DPP Media CTO Survey:** Almost **three quarters** of CTOs were working on transforming their operating model.

### Audience and Trust Shifts
- **Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism:** Younger people on lower incomes with less formal education tend to trust news less, turning instead to social media and influencers.
- **OFCOM (UK):** **71%** of adults now get news online; **70%** obtain news via TV — a generational shift in motion. Revenue has shifted significantly from linear towards digital, yet linear broadcast remains a proven revenue stream.

## Customer Case Study — Large Multinational Broadcaster (Anonymous)

A large multinational broadcaster transitioning from a traditionally siloed organisation (separate departments for broadcast, web, and social) to a unified, story-centric workflow with Wolftech News and MediaCentral.

### Before
- Separate departments for broadcast, web, and social media — minimal coordination
- iNEWS thick client as primary interface
- Disparate communication tools: Teams, Slack, Trello, WhatsApp, email

### Transition Approach
- Began with the broadcast team; expanded to web and social teams
- Key stakeholders engaged collaboratively to demonstrate advantages before rollout
- Gradual, incremental modernisation — no big-bang cutover

### After — Workflow Changes

**Planning and story management:**
Wolftech News is now the primary planning tool for all teams regardless of story type. A Wolftech story is materially more significant than a traditional iNEWS running order story — it carries multiple publishing points and formats, all linked back to a single original story. Users report this is more efficient and easier to track.

**Single pane of glass:**
Journalists use MediaCentral | Cloud UX with Wolftech News as their primary interface — all necessary tools in one browser-based application. Preparation is handled through the browser rather than the iNEWS client.

**iNEWS retained for broadcast production:**
The iNEWS thick client remains in active use by production staff in the production control room (PCR) for broadcast show output. Traditional linear broadcast remains a key part of their portfolio.

**Scripting and asset workflow:**
Reporters use the Wolftech News script editor in MediaCentral | Cloud UX to write scripts and link video material using the storyboard feature. Drag-and-drop adds content to MediaCentral | Production Management, enabling editors in Media Composer or Adobe Premiere Pro to access and use those assets.

**Communication consolidation:**
Wolftech News replaces the previous reliance on multiple external communication platforms. Editorial teams communicate directly within the same tool on both web and mobile (Wolftech Go app). Reported benefit: significant time saved by reducing cross-platform context switching during story development and delivery.

**Automation:**
The MediaCentral Rules Engine, combined with orchestration workflows triggered through MediaCentral | Asset Management, enables:
- Automated creation of multiple story sequences for versioning
- Automated publication to social media platforms
